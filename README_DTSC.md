# Wages, Education & Gender: A Pay Gap Study
**Tools:** Python · SQL · Jupyter Notebook  
**Context:** Data Science & Society — University of North Carolina at Charlotte  
**Team:** Group project (5 members)

---

## 📌 Project Overview

This collaborative research project explores the relationship between educational attainment, gender, and wage outcomes in the American workforce. Specifically, the study investigates whether the gender pay gap narrows, widens, or persists differently between workers with advanced degrees versus those with only a high school diploma — and what that means for equity in the labor market.

---

## ❓ Research Question

> Does higher education reduce the gender wage gap, and how do earnings trajectories differ between male and female workers across education levels?

---

## 🗂️ Dataset

The project uses publicly available wage and education data to examine earnings across demographic groups.

Key variables analyzed:
- Gender (Male / Female)
- Education level (High School Diploma vs. Advanced Degree)
- Annual wages / hourly earnings
- Occupation category *(if applicable)*
- Year / time period

---

## 🔍 Methodology

The analysis combined SQL and Python to explore and model the data:

**SQL** — Used to extract, filter, and aggregate wage data by education level and gender groupings

**Python (Pandas, Matplotlib/Seaborn)** — Used for:
- Data cleaning and preprocessing
- Exploratory data analysis (EDA)
- Visualization of wage distributions and trends
- Regression modeling to quantify the relationship between education, gender, and pay

**Regression Analysis** — Built models to isolate the effect of gender on wages while controlling for education level, surfacing the residual pay gap that persists even with equivalent credentials

---

## 📊 Key Visualizations

- Bar charts comparing median wages by gender and education tier
- Regression plots showing pay gap trends across education levels
- Distribution comparisons of earnings between demographic groups
